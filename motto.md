We are building an interactive course on Differential Geometry. The goal of the course it to teach differential geometry in its full glory through "simulations" which can be understood as advanced vizualization tools(as opposed to toy figures or animations). 

The course is targeted at graduate students in mathematics, physics, and machine learning who have a solid foundation in multivariable calculus and linear algebra, but little to no background in differential geometry. 


Core philosophy: 

1. Emphasize geometric intuition above all else. But do not shy away from the technical details. 
2. Since this topic uses tensors, covariates and contravarients, we use numpy extensively to demmonstrate the machinery such as levi-civita connection, Christoffel symbols and curvature.
3. We use 3d interactive plots to visualize manifolds and the machinery. But do not reduce the topic to a toy.
